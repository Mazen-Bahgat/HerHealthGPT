\begin{abstract}
Large language models are increasingly used for women's-health patient education, yet most evaluations target answer \emph{generation} rather than safe \emph{interpretation} of symptom language across registers and languages.
We present \textbf{HerHealthGPT-LU}, a multilingual benchmark for evaluating LLM understanding of women's-health symptom communication in English, Arabic, and French.
The benchmark comprises 540 style-controlled items spanning menstruation, PCOS/hormonal concerns, and fertility, with gold labels for condition inference, risk calibration, recommended action, and clarification need.
We separate a frozen evaluation benchmark from leakage-controlled adaptation data and propose metrics targeting misunderstanding, unsafe responses, and cross-lingual consistency.
We report baseline and fine-tuned model results together with a systematic error taxonomy for women's-health language understanding.
\end{abstract}
